Microsoft Azure Fundamentals is the friendliest certification in the Azure track — an entry-level exam covering cloud concepts, core Azure services, and Azure's management and governance tools. It requires no hands-on experience and never expires once earned, which makes it an easy, permanent addition to your CV.

## Who should take it

AZ-900 fits anyone who works around Azure: business stakeholders, sales and procurement teams, students, and engineers coming from AWS or on-premises who want a structured introduction to Azure's vocabulary. If you already administer Azure daily, skip ahead to [AZ-104](/exams/az-104).

## How the exam is scored

Expect roughly 40–60 questions in about 45–60 minutes, scored on a 1,000-point scale with 700 needed to pass. Microsoft mixes formats more than other vendors: alongside multiple choice you'll see drag-and-drop matching, dropdown fill-ins, and true/false blocks — our practice mode mirrors these structured formats so they won't surprise you.

## What to focus on

- **Cloud concepts.** IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS, public vs private vs hybrid cloud, capital vs operational expenditure, and the benefits pitch: high availability, scalability, elasticity.
- **Core architecture.** Regions and region pairs, availability zones, resource groups, subscriptions, and management groups — especially the hierarchy between them.
- **Core services.** Virtual Machines, App Service, Azure Functions, Container Instances and AKS at a "what is it for" level, plus storage tiers (hot/cool/archive) and the main database options.
- **Identity and governance.** Microsoft Entra ID, role-based access control, Azure Policy vs RBAC (a favourite trick pairing), resource locks, and tags.
- **Cost and management.** The pricing calculator vs TCO calculator, Cost Management, Azure Monitor, Service Health, and Azure Advisor.

## A realistic study plan

Most people pass AZ-900 with 10–20 hours of study across one to two weeks. Spend the first few days on cloud concepts and the resource hierarchy, then a day on each remaining area, then drill practice questions until you consistently score above 85%. The structured question formats reward familiarity — do enough practice that drag-and-drop matching feels routine.

## Common pitfalls

The classic traps: confusing Azure Policy (controls *what* can be deployed) with RBAC (controls *who* can do things), mixing up availability zones and region pairs, and misremembering which SLA improvements come from availability sets vs zones. Also learn the difference between Azure Monitor, Service Health, and Advisor — all three appear regularly.

## After you pass

AZ-900 never expires. The natural next step for hands-on roles is the [Azure Administrator (AZ-104)](/exams/az-104), which takes the same service catalogue and asks you to actually configure it.
