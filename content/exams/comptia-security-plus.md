CompTIA Security+ is the world's most widely held cybersecurity certification and the de-facto entry ticket to security roles. SY0-701 validates the baseline skills every security job assumes: threats and mitigations, security architecture, operations, and governance. It's also DoD 8140 approved, which makes it effectively mandatory for many US government and contractor positions.

## Who should take it

CompTIA recommends the equivalent of two years of IT experience with a security focus, plus [Network+](/exams/comptia-network-plus)-level knowledge — but Security+ is routinely passed by determined newcomers. It suits helpdesk and sysadmin professionals pivoting to security, developers who need security literacy, and anyone starting a SOC analyst path.

## How the exam is scored

Up to 90 questions in 90 minutes, scored on a scale of 100–900 with a passing score of 750. The exam mixes standard multiple choice with performance-based questions (PBQs) — interactive items where you might match attack types to descriptions, order incident-response steps, or configure a firewall rule set. PBQs usually arrive first and eat time; many candidates flag them and return after the multiple choice.

## What to focus on

- **Threats and mitigations (the biggest domain).** Malware types, social-engineering techniques, attack indicators, and which mitigation answers which attack. Expect scenario questions: "users report X — what happened?"
- **Cryptography fundamentals.** Symmetric vs asymmetric, hashing vs encryption vs signing, PKI and certificate types. You don't need math — you need to know which tool solves which problem.
- **Security operations.** Incident-response phases in order, log analysis, SIEM/SOAR concepts, and forensics basics like chain of custody.
- **Architecture.** Zero trust, segmentation, firewall/IDS/IPS placement, and the cloud shared responsibility model.
- **Governance.** Risk terminology (SLE, ALE, ARO calculations appear), policy types, and the compliance alphabet — GDPR, PCI-DSS, HIPAA — at a recognition level.

## A six-week plan

Weeks 1–2: work through general security concepts and the threats domain — it's the heaviest-weighted and everything else builds on it. Weeks 3–4: architecture and operations, drilling incident response order and log-reading. Week 5: governance plus your first full-length timed practice exam. Week 6: daily mixed practice questions, PBQ drills, and targeted review of your weakest domain. Aim to be scoring 85%+ on fresh questions before booking.

## Common pitfalls

The acronym volume is the number-one complaint — build a running glossary from day one. Candidates also lose marks by memorising attack definitions without being able to recognise them from symptoms, and by leaving PBQs to the end without rehearsing them. Finally, watch the "BEST answer" phrasing: several options are often technically true, and the exam wants the most complete or most immediate action.

## After you pass

Security+ is valid for three years and renews via continuing education. From here, common paths are CySA+ for blue-team work, Pentest+ for offensive security, or — with a few years of experience — the [CISSP](/exams/cissp) for security leadership.
