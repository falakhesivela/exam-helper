CompTIA A+ is the standard first certification for IT careers, and Core 1 (220-1101) is the first of its two required exams. It covers the physical and connected side of IT support: hardware, mobile devices, networking fundamentals, virtualisation, and hardware troubleshooting. Pass Core 1 and Core 2 (220-1102, which covers operating systems and security) and you're A+ certified.

## Who should take it

A+ assumes no prior certification and is designed for people entering IT: helpdesk, desktop support, and field service roles. It's frequently listed as a requirement in entry-level job postings. If you already work in IT and are heading toward networking or security, you may prefer to start directly with [Network+](/exams/comptia-network-plus) or [Security+](/exams/comptia-security-plus) — but A+ remains the gentlest on-ramp.

## How the exam is scored

Up to 90 questions in 90 minutes, scored 100–900 with a pass mark of 675 — the lowest bar of the CompTIA trio, though the sheer breadth of content makes up for it. Expect multiple choice plus a few performance-based questions, such as matching cable types to uses or building a PC configuration to a spec.

## What to focus on

- **Hardware (the heart of the exam).** RAM types, storage (SATA vs NVMe, RAID levels), CPU sockets and features, power supplies, and expansion cards. Expect "a user needs X — which component?" questions.
- **Cables, ports, and connectors.** USB versions and speeds, display connectors, and network cable categories. Pure memorisation, and pure free marks once drilled.
- **Mobile devices.** Laptop component replacement, display technologies, and mobile connectivity and synchronisation.
- **Networking basics.** Common ports (a preview of Network+), Wi-Fi standards, DHCP/DNS at a concept level, and SOHO router configuration.
- **Virtualisation and cloud.** Hypervisor types, VM requirements, and cloud models at a definition level.
- **Troubleshooting methodology.** CompTIA's official troubleshooting steps, *in order* — questions test the sequence explicitly.

## How to prepare

Give yourself four to six weeks. The material isn't deep, but it is wide, so the risk is forgetting early topics by the time you finish late ones — daily mixed-topic practice questions solve this better than chapter-by-chapter cramming. If you can, open a real PC: identifying RAM, storage, and expansion slots by sight makes hardware questions concrete.

## Common pitfalls

Candidates trip on speed/spec memorisation (USB and Wi-Fi standards especially), skip the troubleshooting-step order because it "seems obvious", and underestimate the printer questions — laser printing stages appear on nearly every exam. Drill all three areas explicitly.

## After you pass

Remember Core 1 is only half of A+ — book 220-1102 while the study momentum is fresh. After A+, the classic progression is [Network+](/exams/comptia-network-plus) then [Security+](/exams/comptia-security-plus), each building directly on the last.
