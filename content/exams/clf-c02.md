The AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner is AWS's foundational certification. It validates a big-picture understanding of the AWS cloud — what the major services do, how pricing and support work, and who is responsible for what under the shared responsibility model. No hands-on experience is required, which makes it the standard first step for non-engineers and career changers alike.

## Who should take it

CLF-C02 is designed for people in sales, finance, project management, and leadership who work alongside cloud teams — and for aspiring engineers who want a structured on-ramp before tackling an associate-level exam. If you already work hands-on with AWS daily, you can usually skip straight to the [Solutions Architect Associate](/exams/saa-c03).

## How the exam is scored

You'll face 65 questions in 90 minutes, scored on a 100–1,000 scale with a passing mark of 700. The questions are shorter and more direct than associate-level exams — mostly "which service does X?" and "under the shared responsibility model, who handles Y?"

## What to focus on

- **The shared responsibility model.** Expect several questions. The rule of thumb: AWS is responsible for security *of* the cloud (hardware, facilities, managed-service patching); you are responsible for security *in* the cloud (your data, IAM, configurations).
- **Service identification.** You need breadth, not depth. Know what EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, CloudFront, Route 53, IAM, CloudWatch, and CloudTrail are for, and the one-line difference between similar services.
- **Pricing and support plans.** Understand on-demand vs Reserved Instances vs Savings Plans vs Spot, what the free tier includes, and the differences between Basic, Developer, Business, and Enterprise support.
- **The Well-Architected Framework.** Learn the six pillars by name and a one-sentence description of each.

## A realistic two-week plan

Week one: work through the fundamentals — cloud concepts, the global infrastructure (Regions, Availability Zones, edge locations), and the core service catalogue. Week two: drill practice questions daily, review every wrong answer, and re-read the pricing and support material the day before the exam. Most candidates need 15–25 hours of total study.

## Common pitfalls

The classic mistakes are mixing up CloudWatch (metrics and monitoring) with CloudTrail (API audit logs), confusing Regions with Availability Zones, and not knowing which services are global (IAM, Route 53, CloudFront) versus regional. These distinctions are free marks if you drill them.

## After you pass

The certificate is valid for three years, and passing any associate-level exam automatically renews it. Most engineers continue to the [Solutions Architect Associate](/exams/saa-c03); the jump in difficulty is real but very manageable with the foundation CLF-C02 gives you.
